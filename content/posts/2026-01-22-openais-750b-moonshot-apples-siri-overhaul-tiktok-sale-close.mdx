## üí∞ The Valuation Wars: OpenAI vs Anthropic's Race to Infinity

The two leading AI labs are both sprinting to raise capital at valuations that would have seemed absurd 18 months ago. But the details reveal very different stories. 

### üöÄ OpenAI's $750B Moonshot

[Bloomberg reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-21/openai-s-altman-meets-mideast-investors-for-50-billion-round) that Sam Altman recently visited the Middle East for early talks with investors, aiming to raise $50B+ at a valuation of $750B-$830B. 

**The Context:** This would be the largest private funding round in history, dwarfing Anthropic's $25B+ round currently in progress. For reference, OpenAI CFO Sarah Friar [revealed last week](https://openai.com/index/a-business-that-scales-with-the-value-of-intelligence) that the company hit $20B+ in annualized revenue and now consumes ~1.9 GW of compute. 

**The Enterprise Push:** [The Information reports](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openai-names-former-tml-staffer-zoph-oversee-enterprise-push) that Barret Zoph, the Thinking Machines Lab co-founder who rejoined OpenAI last week, will lead OpenAI's enterprise push as part of a broader reorganization. Basically, OpenAI is betting big on B2B to justify that valuation. 

### üìâ Anthropic's $9B Revenue... and Margin Squeeze

**The Good News:** [Bloomberg reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-21/anthropic-s-revenue-run-rate-tops-9-billion-as-vcs-pile-in) that Anthropic's revenue run rate hit $9B+ at the end of 2025, up from $4B in July. Iconiq, Lightspeed, and Menlo are set to join its new funding round. 

**The Not So Good News:** [The Information reports](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/anthropic-lowers-profit-margin-projection-revenue-skyrockets) that Anthropic projected a 40% gross margin from selling AI to companies and developers in 2025, down from its earlier estimate of 50%, due to 23% higher inference costs. 

**Translation:** Revenue is exploding, but so are the costs of actually running the models. When inference costs jump 23%, and margins drop 10 points, the unit economics of AI aren't quite as magical as the top-line growth suggests. Both companies are racing to raise at astronomical valuations, but only one of them is showing margin compression.

## üçé Apple's AI Awakening: Siri Finally Gets a Brain

Photo by [appshunter.io](https://appshunter.io) on [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com/photos/a-close-up-of-a-cell-phone-with-a-keyboard-on-it-4uup67zqH9k?utm_source=unsplash&utm_medium=referral&utm_content=creditCopyText)

After years of watching ChatGPT eat its lunch, Apple is finally treating AI as an existential threat rather than a feature update. 

### ü§ñ Siri Becomes a Chatbot

[Mark Gurman reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-21/ios-27-apple-to-revamp-siri-as-built-in-iphone-mac-chatbot-to-fend-off-openai) that Apple plans to revamp Siri this fall in iOS 27 and macOS 27, turning it into the company's first AI chatbot and replacing the current Siri interface entirely. 

**The Federighi Factor:** [The Information adds](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/google-deal-apples-craig-federighi-plots-cautious-course-ai) that Craig Federighi led a push to use other companies' models to help launch the revamped Siri. This explains the Gemini deal we covered last week, so Apple needed external help to catch up. 

### üìç The Hardware Play: AI Wearable Pin

[The Information also reports](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/apple-developing-ai-wearable-pin) that Apple is developing an AirTag-sized AI wearable pin with cameras, a speaker, microphones, and wireless charging, which it could launch as early as 2027. 

**The Competition:** This puts Apple in direct competition with OpenAI's rumored screenless wearable, expected in H2 2026. The race to build the post-smartphone AI device is officially on. 

**Translation:** Apple spent years treating Siri as "good enough." Now they're rebuilding it from scratch AND building dedicated AI hardware. When the world's most valuable company goes into catch-up mode, you know the competitive landscape has shifted permanently. 

## üéØ TikTok Endgame + The Great AI Talent Exodus

Photo by [Solen Feyissa](https://unsplash.com/@solenfeyissa?utm_source=unsplash&utm_medium=referral&utm_content=creditCopyText) on [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com/photos/person-holding-black-iphone-5-Yaw9mfG9QfQ?utm_source=unsplash&utm_medium=referral&utm_content=creditCopyText)

Two stories that seemed impossible a year ago are now happening simultaneously: TikTok is getting sold, and the researchers who built the AI revolution are leaving to start their own companies. 

### üì± TikTok US Sale Finally Closing

[Semafor reports](https://www.semafor.com/article/01/22/2026/china-us-sign-off-on-tiktok-us-spinoff) that the US and China have both approved a deal to sell TikTok's US business to a group of mostly US investors led by Oracle and Silverlake, set to close this week. 

**The North American Sweep:** Meanwhile,[ a Canadian federal court overturned](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/01/21/canada-tiktok-court-ruling-00739237) a 2024 order that would have shuttered TikTok Canada's business operations over national security concerns. TikTok survives on both sides of the border. 

### üß† AI Researchers Fleeing Big Labs

The talent that built these AI giants is increasingly betting they can do it better on their own. 

**Yann LeCun 's Exit:**[ MIT Technology Review published a Q&A;](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/22/1131661/yann-lecuns-new-venture-ami-labs/) with Yann LeCun about his new Paris-based startup, Advanced Machine Intelligence (AMI Labs), his departure from Meta, and his plans for world models and robotics. 

**The $480M Seed:**[ The New York Times reports](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/20/technology/humans-ai-anthropic-xai.html) that Humans&, founded by ex-Anthropic, xAI, and Google staff to build collaborative AI, raised a $480M seed from Nvidia, Jeff Bezos, and others at a $4.48B valuation. 

**The OpenAI Critique:**[ Core Memory published a Q&A;](https://www.corememory.com/p/he-left-openai-jerry-tworek) with recently departed OpenAI VP of Research Jerry Tworek, who claims OpenAI's shift toward more conservative approaches made high-risk, pioneering work harder. 

**Translation:** When Yann LeCun leaves Meta, a team of ex-Anthropic/xAI/Google researchers raises half a billion at seed, and OpenAI's former VP of Research says the company got too conservative, and the talent is voting with its feet. The next generation of AI labs is being founded right now.
