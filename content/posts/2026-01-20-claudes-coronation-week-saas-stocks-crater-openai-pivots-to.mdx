## üëë Claude's Coronation: Anthropic's Breakout Week

[Claude: Cowork: Claude Code for the rest of your work](https://claude.com/blog/cowork-research-preview)

### üìà The Numbers That Matter

[Similarweb data shows Claude's web audience more than doubled YoY in December 2025](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/anthropic-claude-code-ai-7a46460e), with the WSJ reporting that many coders spent their holiday breaks on a "Claude bender" testing Claude Opus 4.5. That's not marketing; that's developers voting with their keyboards. 

**The Fundraise:**[Sources tell the Financial Times](https://www.ft.com/content/53220829-2ab2-471c-9a00-30d24beb8d48) that Sequoia is planning a major investment in Anthropic, joining a round led by GIC and Coatue (investing $1.5B each) as the company aims to raise $25B+. For context, that would be one of the largest private funding rounds in history. 

**The Product Expansion:**[Anthropic opened Claude Cowork to $20/month Pro subscribers](https://www.engadget.com/ai/anthropic-opens-up-its-claude-cowork-feature-to-anyone-with-a-20-subscription-194000021.html) this week, after initially launching it only for Max users. The caveat is that Pro users "may hit their usage limits sooner." So it seems that demand is outstripping capacity. 

**The Talent Magnet:**[Andrea Vallone, OpenAI's former Head of Model Policy](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/862402/openai-safety-lead-model-policy-departs-for-anthropic-alignment-andrea-vallone), joined Anthropic's alignment team. When your competitor's safety leadership keeps defecting to you, the market is sending a message. 

### üíÄ The SaaS Reckoning

Claude's rise has a body count: software stocks. [Bloomberg reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-18/-no-reasons-to-own-software-stocks-sink-on-fear-of-new-ai-tool) that Claude Cowork has revived fears about AI disruption, with Morgan Stanley's SaaS index now down 15% YTD. [Doug O'Laughlin at Fabricated Knowledge](https://www.fabricatedknowledge.com/p/the-death-of-software-20-a-better) called it an "extinction-level event" for horizontal software companies. Adobe's stock has slumped 45%+ since the end of 2023, with [analysts now the most bearish since 2013](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-13/adobe-analysts-turn-most-bearish-since-2013-as-ai-threat-looms). 

**Translation:** The market isn't just pricing in competition; it's pricing in obsolescence. When a SaaS index drops 15% in three weeks because of one product launch, investors are betting that AI agents don't just compete with software; they replace the need for it.

## üì∫ OpenAI's Advertising Pivot: ChatGPT Gets Commercials

[OpenAI: Our approach to advertising and expanding access to ChatGPT](https://openai.com/index/our-approach-to-advertising-and-expanding-access/)

OpenAI is done pretending subscriptions are enough. 

### üì¢ The Ad Play

[The Financial Times reports](https://t.co/qWMhWBYbKz) that OpenAI plans to test ads beneath ChatGPT replies for users on the free and Go tiers in the US. A source says OpenAI expects to generate "low billions" in ad revenue in 2026. [Wired clarifies](https://www.wired.com/story/openai-testing-ads-us/) that ads will match conversation topics using personalization data, but OpenAI claims it won't sell user data or expose conversations to advertisers. 

**The Global Push:**[OpenAI expanded ChatGPT Go to the US and rest of the world](https://www.theverge.com/news/863466/openai-chatgpt-go-global-release) this week at $8/month, creating a new tier between free (with ads) and Pro ($20/month). 

**The Scale Context:** OpenAI CFO Sarah Friar [revealed](https://openai.com/index/a-business-that-scales-with-the-value-of-intelligence) that compute grew from 0.2 GW in 2023 to ~1.9 GW in 2025, while annualized revenue grew from $2B to $20B+. At that burn rate, ads aren't optional; they're necessary. 

### üîß The Hardware Angle

[OpenAI issued RFPs to US hardware manufacturers](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-15/openai-seeks-us-based-suppliers-for-planned-robotics-ai-device-push) for consumer devices, robotics, and data centers. Executive Chris Lehane [confirmed](https://www.axios.com/2026/01/19/openai-device-2026-lehane-jony-ive) the company is "on track" to unveil its first device in H2 2026, with reports suggesting it will be a screenless wearable. 

**Translation:** OpenAI's business model is evolving from "charge power users" to "monetize everyone." Ads for free users, $8 for casual users, $20 for pro users, $200 for power users, and eventually hardware. They're not building a chatbot; they're building an ecosystem. 

## üí• Thinking Machines Lab: The Wheels Come Off

[Bloomberg Originals: Inside OpenAI, the Architect of ChatGPT, featuring Mira Murati | The Circuit with Emily Chang](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p9Q5a1Vn-Hk)

What started as a promising AI lab is turning into a cautionary tale about founder drama. 

### üö™ The Exodus

[The Information reports](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/thinking-machines-exodus-tests-investor-appetite-50-billion-valuation) that after five staff left, investors are rattled, potentially impacting the company's $50B valuation fundraise. The kicker: two researchers quit via Slack during an all-hands meeting. [Sources tell Alex Heath](https://sources.news/p/thinking-machines-is-nothing-without) the company lacks a clear product or business strategy. 

**The CTO Drama:**[Wired reported](https://www.wired.com/story/thinking-machines-lab-cofounder-office-relationship-firing-openai/) that leaders confronted co-founder Barret Zoph over an alleged relationship with another employee in the summer of 2025. Zoph has since returned to OpenAI, along with two other co-founders. [At least two more employees](https://www.wired.com/story/inside-openai-raid-on-thinking-machines-lab/) are expected to follow, with some researchers citing exhaustion from "the industry's constant drama."

**Translation:** Thinking Machines went from $50B valuation target to an existential crisis in weeks. When co-founders leave for the competition, researchers quit during all-hands, and investors smell blood, the product doesn't matter.
