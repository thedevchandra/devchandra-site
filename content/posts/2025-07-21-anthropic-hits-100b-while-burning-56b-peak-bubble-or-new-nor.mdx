### ü§ñ Anthropic's $100B Moment: The Claude Effect

This week, Anthropic finds itself in an unusual position: [investors are chasing the company with $100B valuation offers](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-16/anthropic-draws-investor-interest-at-more-than-100-billion-valuation), even though the AI startup isn't actively fundraising. Just months ago, they were valued at $61.5B. 

  * **The Numbers** : Revenue exploded from $3B to $4B annualized in just one month, with Claude Code alone generating $200M+. At $100B, they'd trade at 25x revenue, aggressive but more reasonable than OpenAI's 50x multiple. 

  * **The Catch?** They burned through $5.6B in 2024 while remaining unprofitable. User metrics are flattening despite strong enterprise adoption, and the talent wars with Meta and OpenAI threaten momentum. 

  * **Why the Frenzy?** Anthropic has positioned itself as the credible OpenAI alternative for enterprises. Their specialized products, such as Claude Code or Claude for Financial Services, demonstrate that they're targeting high-value verticals with premium pricing. 




Burning $5.6B to generate $4B in revenue isn't a business model. It's a bet that scale will eventually lead to profitability. And the fact that investors are approaching them, rather than Anthropic seeking funding, suggests the market has more capital than sense. 

### üîç Perplexity's $18B Reality Check: When Search Gets an AI Makeover

This week, [Perplexity AI raised another $100M, pushing its valuation to $18B](https://techfundingnews.com/perplexity-ai-18b-valuation-funding-comet-browser-launch/). However, here's the kicker: this wasn't even a new round, but rather an extension of their previous raise, as investors couldn't secure enough funding. 

  * **The Numbers** : Revenue rocketed from $35M to $150M annualized, while valuation soared from under $1B to $18B in less than two years. That's a 120x revenue multiple for those keeping score. 

  * **The Strategy** : Launched Comet, an AI-powered browser exclusive to premium subscribers, and inked a deal with Indian telecom giant Bharti Airtel, giving 360M+ users free Perplexity Pro for a year. 

  * **The Competition** : Taking on Google in search is like bringing a knife to a gunfight, but Perplexity's betting that citation-rich AI answers beat traditional blue links. 




At $150M in revenue and an $18B valuation, Perplexity is trading at multiples that make even Anthropic look reasonably priced. However, whether AI search is worth 120x revenue remains the billion-dollar question. 

## üèõÔ∏è Crypto Week: Congress Finally Gives Digital Assets the Rulebook They've Been Begging For

### üíµ The Stablecoin Slam Dunk

[Trump signed the GENIUS Act into law on July 18](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/07/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-signs-genius-act-into-law/), creating the first federal framework for dollar-pegged stablecoins. The highlights: 

  * **100% reserve requirement** backed by cash or short-term Treasuries (no more Tether-style "trust us, bro" accounting) 

  * **Monthly public disclosures** on what's actually backing your digital dollars 

  * **Consumer protections** that give holders priority if an issuer goes bust 

  * **Full AML/sanctions compliance** because Congress wants their cut of the action 




### üéØ The Great Regulatory Turf War Gets Settled

[The CLARITY Act passed the House 294-134](https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2025/06/bipartisan-majorities-in-two-house-committees-vote-to-advance-the-digital-asset-market-clarity-act-of-2025) and aims to finally answer the eternal question: Is it a security or a commodity? 

  * **CFTC gets the win** for most digital commodities, while SEC keeps investment contracts 

  * **Three-tier token taxonomy** lets tokens "mature" from securities to commodities once they're decentralized enough 

  * **Federal exchange registration** means no more state-by-state licensing hell for crypto exchanges 




### üö´ The Anti-CBDC Middle Finger

[The House narrowly passed (219-217) the Anti-CBDC Surveillance State Act](https://financialservices.house.gov/uploadedfiles/2025-07-10_---_anti-cbdc_one-pager_final.pdf), basically telling the Fed to keep its hands off programmable money: 

  * **No retail CBDC** without explicit Congressional approval 

  * **Can 't use it for monetary policy**: sorry, negative interest rate dreams 

  * **Privacy protection** because nobody wants their coffee purchases being tracked 




After years of regulatory uncertainty, crypto finally gets adult supervision. Stablecoins gain legitimacy through strict rules, the industry receives clarity, consumers receive protection, and Congress gets to pretend it understands blockchain. 

But the real winner? Lawyers, who now have 500+ pages of new regulations to bill against.
