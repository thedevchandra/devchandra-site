### üìà Circle‚Äôs NYSE IPO Goes Lunar

**Stablecoin** giant Circle's shares climbed 48% on Friday, extending the stock's blockbuster NYSE debut, which saw the [shares nearly quadruple from their $31 offer price](https://www.reuters.com/business/stablecoin-firm-circle-scales-record-high-after-blockbuster-nyse-listing-2025-06-06/) to a high of $123.49. The stellar performance is indicating the IPO market's recovery, with analysts noting that companies less exposed to tariff uncertainties are finding strong investor appetite. 

**More Context** : As last month, the IPO market was pretty dull, with only [five companies going public at valuations above $1B](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/data-global-monthly-funding-may-2025/?utm_source=cb_weekend&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=20250607): 

  1. **eToro:** Israel-based social trading platform 

  2. **Hinge Health:** San Francisco-based digital clinic 

  3. **Shanghai Auntie:** Shanghai-based tea company 

  4. **Ather Energy:** India-based electric scooter manufacturer 

  5. **MNTN:** Austin, Texas-based advertising platform 




**Translation** : Wall Street seems optimistic about IPO momentum, especially in sectors such as AI, defense, and fintech, which are shielded from international trade risks. 

### üåé LatAm Capital Shows Promise

### üíº Nascent VC Unveils $15 M Operator-Led Fund II

Mexico's Nascent VC, backed by the founders of Kavak, Bitso, and Belvo,[ has raised its second fund of $15M](https://contxto.com/en/venture-capital-es/operator-led-nascent-vc-announces-the-launch-of-its-15-million-fund-ii/). Being 7x larger than the debut vehicle, Fund II aims to write $500K checks into "pre-PowerPoint" stage fintech, proptech, e-commerce, and AI startups. The first fund has already delivered a 6.5x gross return with 5.2x distributed to investors. 

**Translation** : The Latam space is one not to sleep on as these boots-on-the-ground operators are doubling down on early-stage Latin America talent.
