## üèõÔ∏è Trump's AI Policy Takeover: When Silicon Valley Gets Its Own Regulator

[The White House: Ensuring a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/12/eliminating-state-law-obstruction-of-national-artificial-intelligence-policy/)

### üìú The Executive Order: One Ring to Rule Them All

President Trump signed an EO[ aimed at preempting state AI laws](https://www.reuters.com/world/trump-says-he-will-sign-order-curbing-state-ai-laws-2025-12-11/), declaring "we want to have one central source of approval:"

  * **OMB Guidance:** New rules require[ LLMs procured by federal agencies to comply with "truth-seeking" and "ideological neutrality" principles](https://www.axios.com/2025/12/11/white-house-woke-ai-guidance-federal-agencies)

  * **Enforcement Mechanism:** The EO[ orders AG Pam Bondi to create an "AI Litigation Task Force"](https://www.engadget.com/ai/trump-orders-creation-of-litigation-task-force-to-challenge-state-ai-laws-022657022.html) that meets regularly with David Sacks to challenge state laws 

  * **The Threat:** [Directs federal agencies to consider withholding funding](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2025/12/11/trump-executive-order-ai-states/) from states passing "the most onerous and excessive laws" around AI 




### üé≠ Sacks Plays Defense While MAGA Plays Offense

AI czar David Sacks defended the order,[ telling Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-12-12/white-house-ai-czar-defends-trump-push-to-rein-in-state-rules) it aims to "ease companies' compliance burden" and that the administration is working with Congress on a common oversight standard. 

**The Behind-the-Scenes Drama:**[ Sacks and AI adviser Sriram Krishnan had to overcome significant MAGA opposition](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2025/12/14/trump-ai-executive-order-divisions/) to get the EO signed, spending weeks talking to lawmakers and ultimately modifying the language to get it across the finish line. 

### üó≥Ô∏è States to DC: We'll Do What We Want

The reception from state capitals ranged from eye-rolls to open defiance.[ Policymakers behind key state AI bills scoffed at the EO](https://www.axios.com/2025/12/12/trump-states-defiant-ai-executive-order), with some GOP governors pushing ahead with their own legislation anyway. Steve Bannon publicly claimed Sacks "misled Trump" on the order's implications. 

**The Plot Twist:** New York's governor is[ proposing to rewrite the RAISE Act using text copied verbatim from California's SB 53](https://www.transformernews.ai/p/new-york-governor-hochul-raise-act-sb-53): the exact kind of cross-state regulatory coordination the EO was designed to prevent. 

**The Legal Time Bomb:** As[ Techdirt points out](https://www.techdirt.com/2025/12/12/trump-pretends-to-block-state-ai-laws-media-pretends-thats-legal/), the EO's interstate commerce argument could backfire spectacularly by invalidating GOP state laws targeting "Big Tech censorship." If the feds can preempt California's AI safety rules, they can preempt Texas's content moderation mandates too. 

**The Bottom Line:** The administration has created a federal AI czar with a taxpayer-funded legal team to sue states, while simultaneously demanding that AI models be "ideologically neutral." However, the president's own base is skeptical, and states are already copying each other's homework in defiance. 

## ü§ñ OpenAI's Big Week: New Models, Mouse Ears, and Adult Mode

[OpenAI Launches GPT-5.2](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-2)

### üß† GPT-5.2: The "Best Model Yet" (Again)

OpenAI[ debuted GPT-5.2](https://www.wired.com/story/openai-gpt-launch-gemini-code-red/) in three modes (Instant, Thinking, and Pro) with improvements across writing, coding, and reasoning benchmarks: 

  * **API Pricing:**[ $1.75/1M input and $14/1M output tokens](https://x.com/openaidevs/status/1999184802755354954); Pro tier at $21/1M input and $168/1M output 

  * **Context Window:**[ 400K tokens in, 128K max output](https://simonw.substack.com/p/gpt-52-and-useful-patterns-for-building), with an August 31, 2025 knowledge cutoff 

  * **The Benchmark Flex:** OpenAI says[ GPT-5.2 Thinking beats or ties industry professionals](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-2) on 70.9% of GDPval knowledge work tasks at 11x the speed and less than 1% of the cost 

  * **Hallucination Claims:**[ Hallucinates less than GPT-5.1](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/842529/openai-gpt-5-2-new-model-chatgpt); prerelease testers include Notion, Box, Shopify, and Zoom 




**Translation:** OpenAI claims it's their most capable model to date, a claim they've made about every release since GPT-3. Their pitch is that GPT-5.2 can do your job faster and cheaper than you can. 

### üè∞ Disney Brings 200 Characters to Sora (And $1B to OpenAI)

[Disney and OpenAI signed a three-year deal](https://openai.com/index/disney-sora-agreement/) bringing 200+ Disney characters to Sora, with Disney making a $1B investment in OpenAI. It's the biggest validation yet for AI-generated video, and an admission that Sora desperately needs content people actually want to create. 

**The Engagement Problem:**[ Sora has high costs and limited traction](https://www.ft.com/content/b14490d9-3ac9-45ce-bce5-df6c39db472f), with users averaging just 13 minutes per day versus 90 minutes on TikTok. OpenAI hopes that letting users generate Baby Yoda doing the macarena will fix the retention crisis. 

**The Control Structure:** The deal[ gives Disney significant oversight](https://www.axios.com/2025/12/11/openai-disney-sora), including a joint steering committee to monitor user creations. Bob Iger insists the partnership[ "does not in any way represent a threat to creators"](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/bob-iger-openai-deal-paramount-netflix-warner-bros-1236448029/)‚Äîthe kind of reassurance that historically precedes exactly that. 

**Meanwhile, Google Gets the Stick:** Disney[ sent Google a cease-and-desist letter](https://variety.com/2025/digital/news/disney-google-ai-copyright-infringement-cease-and-desist-letter-1236606429/) accusing it of "massive scale" copyright infringement for AI-generated Disney content on YouTube. Google promptly[ removed dozens of AI videos depicting Disney characters](https://variety.com/2025/film/news/google-removes-ai-videos-disney-characters-cease-desist-1236608015/).

## üé∞ Crypto's Regulatory Arbitrage: From Enforcement to Enablement

[The White House: Crypto](https://www.whitehouse.gov/crypto/)

### üèõÔ∏è Washington Rolls Out the Welcome Mat

The SEC's crypto enforcement machine has gone from attack dog to lap dog. A NYT investigation found the agency[ eased up on or dismissed 60%+ of ongoing crypto cases](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/14/us/politics/sec-crypto-firms-trump-investigation.html) after Trump's return to office, including investigations into Trump-linked companies. 

**The CFTC Joins the Party:** The Commodity Futures Trading Commission[ withdrew its 2020 guidance on "actual delivery" of digital assets](https://bitcoinmagazine.com/markets/cftc-scraps-overly-complex-crypto-rules), removing rules the agency now calls "overly complex" that had required physical settlement of crypto trades within 28 days. 

**Translation:** The regulators who spent 2022-2024 treating crypto like organized crime are now treating it like a campaign donor. Which, to be fair, it was. 

### üîÆ Prediction Markets Go Mainstream (With All the Problems That Implies)

The CFTC[ granted Gemini a Designated Contract Market license](https://www.theblock.co/post/382142/gemini-cftc-approval-prediction-market-crypto-futures-options-perps) for its prediction markets platform Gemini Titan, allowing it to offer regulated crypto futures, options, and perpetuals alongside event contracts. The Winklevoss twins finally have their casino license. 

**The Volume War:**[ Kalshi integrated into the Phantom crypto wallet](http://www.forbes.com/sites/digital-assets/2025/12/12/kalshi-challenges-polymarkets-crypto-connections-launching-tokenized-event-trading-via-phantom-wallet/) and is winning the prediction markets arms race. November trading volume reached $5.8B, compared with Polymarket's $3.7B. When you can bet on OpenAI's next release date from the same wallet holding your Solana, the user acquisition funnel gets very short. 

**The Insider Trading Problem:**[ Companies are scrambling to update insider trading policies](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/polymarket-bets-openai-google-raise-insider-trading-suspicions) to address prediction markets following suspicious betting patterns around OpenAI and Google announcements. Kalshi is pushing for federal oversight, a rare case of an industry seeking regulation because the alternative is losing institutional customers due to compliance concerns. 

**Reality Check:** Prediction markets spent years arguing they shouldn't be regulated like gambling. Now that they're mainstream, they're discovering why gambling is regulated: people cheat. 

### üèúÔ∏è The Gulf State Gold Rush

[Michael Saylor, Changpeng Zhao, and a parade of crypto executives descended on Abu Dhabi](https://www.wsj.com/finance/currencies/crypto-industry-persian-gulf-states-22a0d3a1) this week, hoping to secure deals with Emirati investors. The UAE has positioned itself as crypto's Switzerland: friendly jurisdiction, deep pockets, and a notable lack of extradition treaties with the US. 

**The Timing:** CZ, freshly pardoned by Trump after his Bank Secrecy Act conviction, is back on the conference circuit, courting sovereign wealth. Saylor's pitching MicroStrategy's Bitcoin treasury strategy to funds managing hundreds of billions. The message is clear: if US regulation gets uncomfortable again, there's always the Gulf. 

**The Bottom Line:** Crypto's regulatory strategy has evolved from "move fast and break things" to "move money to wherever the rules are friendliest."
