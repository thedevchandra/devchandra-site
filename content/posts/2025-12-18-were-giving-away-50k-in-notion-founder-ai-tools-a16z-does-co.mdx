## üèõÔ∏è DC‚Äôs AI Awakening: When VCs Write Policy and Tech Bros Join Government

[A16z: A Roadmap for Federal AI Legislation: Protect People, Empower Builders, Win the Future](https://www.a16z.news/p/a-roadmap-for-federal-ai-legislation)

### üìú a16z's Nine Pillars: A VC Firm Writes Congress's Homework

[Andreessen Horowitz published "A Roadmap for Federal AI Legislation"](https://www.a16z.news/p/a-roadmap-for-federal-ai-legislation), a comprehensive policy framework that reads less like a think-tank white paper and more like draft legislation waiting for a sponsor with 9 pillars: 

  1. **Punish Harmful Uses:** AI shouldn't be a "get out of jail free" card‚Äîexisting criminal, civil rights, and consumer protection laws should apply 

  2. **Protect Children:** Prohibit AI use for under-13s without parental consent; require crisis protocols for self-harm situations 

  3. **National Security:** Direct federal agencies to test and benchmark CBRN and cyber capabilities with evidence-based assessments 

  4. **Transparency Standards:** Require "AI model facts" disclosures‚Äîwho built it, when, intended uses, supported languages, and terms of service 

  5. **Federal Leadership:** Congress regulates model development; states retain authority over harmful _uses_ within their borders 

  6. **Talent Investment:** Modernize the 80-year-old National Apprenticeship Act; implement AI literacy in K-12 curricula 

  7. **Infrastructure Spending:** Create a National AI Competitiveness Institute (NAICI) offering compute access, curated datasets, and benchmarking tools 

  8. **Research Funding:** Prioritize "moonshot" high-risk, high-reward projects across near, medium, and long-term horizons 

  9. **Government Modernization:** Every federal agency should develop time-bound AI implementation plans 




**The "Little Tech" Angle:** a16z frames the entire document around protecting startups from regulatory burdens that favor deep-pocketed incumbents. As they put it, quoting former White House deputy CTO Jennifer Pahlka: "Paperwork favors the powerful."

**Translation:** When the most influential VC firm in tech hands Congress a fully drafted AI policy framework complete with constitutional analysis and implementation details, it's either lobbying genius or public service, depending on your view of whether "protect builders" and "protect people" can coexist in the same sentence. 

### üéñÔ∏è Tech Force: Building Government Tech After DOGE Burned It Down

[The Trump administration launched the US Tech Force](https://techforce.gov/), recruiting an "elite corps" of ~1,000 engineers from Big Tech for two-year government modernization stints: 

  * **Pay:** $150k-$200k annually, employed directly by agencies 

  * **Participating Companies:** Palantir, Meta, Oracle, xAI, and about 20 others signed on 

  * **Timeline:** Initial cohort could start as early as March 

  * **Administration:** NobleReach Foundation is helping run the program 

  * **Placement:** Engineers stationed across War, Labor, IRS, and other agencies 




**The Irony Nobody 's Ignoring:**[ This comes after DOGE drove out thousands of federal tech workers](https://www.nextgov.com/people/2025/12/trump-admin-launches-us-tech-force-recruit-temporary-workers-after-shedding-thousands-year/410159/): 

  * GSA dismantled 18F (the internal tech consultancy) in March after Musk posted it had been "deleted."

  * The Social Security Administration closed its Office of Transformation. 

  * The Defense Digital Service collapsed after mass resignations. 

  * The IRS lost 2,000+ tech workers by June. 




**The Conflict Question:** Private sector employees will take "leaves of absence" to become full-time government employees, but won't be required to divest their stock holdings. OPM Director Scott Kupor insists they've "run down all the various conflict issues," but critics note this setup could "recreate some of the worst aspects of early DOGE" by bringing in people who don't understand public sector legal constraints. 

**The Pitch:** "Come work on literally the world's most complex and difficult problems," says Kupor. "There is no bigger and more complex set of problems than we face in the federal government."

**Reality Check:** The Tech Force website states it has no "political mission," a curious disclaimer for a program launched by an administration that used DOGE to access sensitive government data and systems, prompting multiple lawsuits. 

## ü§ñ OpenAI's Paradox: Record Revenue, Rising Tensions, Astronomical Valuations

[OpenAI: Evaluating AI‚Äôs ability to perform scientific research tasks](https://openai.com/index/frontierscience/)

### üí∞ The $750B Fundraising Circus: Two Deals, One Company

OpenAI's fundraising ambitions have entered the realm of the surreal, with two potential mega-rounds reportedly in the works that could coexist or compete: 

**Track 1: The Amazon Strategic Deal:**[Amazon is in early talks to invest $10B+ in OpenAI](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openai-talks-raise-least-10-billion-amazon-use-ai-chips) at a $500B+ valuation, structured as a strategic partnership: 

  * **Trainium Commitment:** OpenAI would use Amazon's custom AI chips to train and run future models 

  * **AWS Deepening:** Builds on existing multibillion-dollar cloud agreement 

  * **No Resale Rights:** Amazon can't offer OpenAI models to AWS customers and Microsoft retains exclusive commercialization rights 

  * **Amazon 's Upside:** Chip utilization, AI workload gravity on AWS, strategic alignment 




**Track 2: The Massive General Raise:**[Separately, OpenAI has held preliminary discussions](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openai-discussed-raising-tens-billions-valuation-around-750-billion) about raising "tens of billions," potentially up to $100B, at around $750B valuation: 

  * **Scale:** Would be among the largest private fundraises in history 

  * **Purpose:** Fund compute, chips, and data center expansion 

  * **Status:** Early talks, subject to change 




**The Math That Breaks Brains:** At $750B, OpenAI would be worth more than Berkshire Hathaway. For a company that has never been profitable and whose competitive moat is being actively eroded by Google, Anthropic, and open-source alternatives, the valuation assumes a future in which OpenAI wins everything. 

### üî• The "Code Red" Behind the Curtain

[Internal tensions between OpenAI's research and ChatGPT product groups](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openais-organizational-problems-hurt-chatgpt) contributed to Sam Altman's "code red" declaration, revealing friction even as the company beats its targets: 

**The Organizational Drama:**

  * **Research vs. Product:** Friction over priorities, resourcing, and how aggressively to chase mass-market monetization 

  * **Resource Reallocation:** "Code red" shifted resources toward upgrading ChatGPT 

  * **Delayed Initiatives:** Advertising plans, AI agents for health/shopping, and "Pulse" personal assistant, all pushed back 

  * **Daily Standups:** Altman established daily calls for ChatGPT improvement teams 




**The Revenue Reality:**

  * **$13B Target:** OpenAI is on track to _exceed_ its 2025 goal 

  * **Internal Projections:** Some forecasts mention potential revenue reaching "far higher" by 2030 

  * **Sustainability Questions:** Heavy spending on computing and data centers raises profitability concerns 

  * **University Land Grab:** [OpenAI has sold 700K+ ChatGPT licenses to ~35 US public universities](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-12-18/schools-ink-chatgpt-copilot-deals-with-students-embracing-ai), rapidly outpacing Copilot despite Microsoft‚Äôs enterprise relationships and Office integration 




**Translation:** OpenAI is printing money while simultaneously panicking about competitive threats and dealing with teams that can't agree on priorities. The company embodies Silicon Valley's eternal truth: success and chaos are not mutually exclusive.
