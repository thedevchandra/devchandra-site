## üèõÔ∏è The White House‚Äôs Bold Play to Dominate & Win the AI Race

[Winning the AI Race Part 5: President Trump on the AI Action Plan](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HmxbPH1PL_A)

The White House just dropped its master plan to dominate AI, and it reads like a Silicon Valley fever dream written by someone who really hates paperwork. 

At yesterday's summit co-hosted with the All-In Podcast crew, Trump unveiled ‚Äú[America's AI Action Plan](https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2025/07/white-house-unveils-americas-ai-action-plan/),‚Äù which is structured around three core pillars: 

### üöÄ 1) Accelerate AI Innovation (While Deleting All the Rules)

  * **Regulation Rollback:** Dismantle "onerous" federal and state-level regulatory barriers. Agencies are directed to fast-track removals within 180 days. 

  * **State Compliance Theater:** States that impose AI restrictions lose federal funding (subtle!) to prevent a "patchwork of 50 different state regulatory regimes."

  * **" Woke AI" Ban:** Federal agencies are directed to use AI that promotes "truth, fairness, and strict impartiality," while avoiding AI that exhibits "partisan bias or ideological agendas."

  * **Open Source Push:** Encourage open models to make US systems the global standard, and seek industry and academic input for further deregulation. 




### ‚ö° 2) Build American AI Infrastructure (aka SimCity: Data Center Edition)

  * **Rapid Buildout:** Expedite permitting and invest massively in data centers, chip fabs, and supporting energy infrastructure. Crusoe's CEO, Chase Lochmiller, noted that AI is "the biggest capital investment in human history."

  * **Energy Emphasis:** As Sec Bessent said, "You can't create new industries without energy," and the admin is "all in" on natural gas and nuclear. This includes allowing companies to build their own power plants and sell excess energy back to the grid. 

  * **Power Plant Free-for-All:** Companies can build their own power plants and sell excess to the grid. 

  * **20% by 2030:** Data centers will consume 20% of US power demand growth in 5 years. 




### ü§ù 3) Dominate Internationally (and Flood Our Tech Worldwide)

  * **Export Controls:** Aggressive new measures to block the export of US AI tech to China and other adversaries, closing loopholes and monitoring third-country rerouting. 

  * **Allied ‚ÄúCoordination:‚Äù** Partners are encouraged to adopt US tech standards or potentially get frozen out, while support will be given to insulate allied supply chains. 

  * **Trade Deals:** Sec Howard Lutnick, a key trade negotiator, highlighted a huge deal with Japan (a $550B "signing bonus") for the US, opening Japan's market and giving the US 90% of project profits. This is seen as a blueprint for future Asian deals. 




### üë∑ Workforce & Economic Impact

  * **Job Creation, Not Displacement:** AI is a "job creation engine," not a destroyer. Chris Power (Hadrian) demonstrated how AI-powered factories are re-industrializing America and creating "thousands of jobs" for people even without prior factory experience. 

  * **Leveling the Playing Field** : Nvidia's CEO, Jensen Huang, stated that AI is a "great equalizer" making "everybody a programmer now" and that "if you're not using AI, you're going to lose your job to somebody who uses AI". 

  * **Talent Development:** Depts of Labor and Education are promoting AI skill development, literacy, and vocational pipelines, leveraging apprenticeships and tax incentives. The SBA has rewritten rules to allow SMBs to use federal loans for AI and manufacturing. 




White House AI & Crypto Czar David Sacks states this is a "global competition or global race," with "unthinkable" consequences for the US if it loses, given AI's ramifications for the economy and national security. 

President Trump himself declared, "America is going to win it". 

Supporters believe it's essential for AI competitiveness and national security. However, critics caution that it prioritizes corporate interests over public safety and ethical oversight.
